# Story And Voiceover

The voiceover is the timeline spine. Write it before detailed animation.

## Beat Design

Each beat should have:

- One purpose.
- One visual setting.
- One primary actor.
- One proof point.
- A clear entry and exit state.

Keep beats short enough that viewers remember the point.

## Voiceover Rules

Good narration is concrete:

- "The system removes already-proven skills."
- "The plan keeps mandatory training protected."
- "The leader sees open gaps by team."

Weak narration is abstract:

- "The platform leverages intelligence."
- "The experience is seamless."
- "The workflow is future-ready."

If the product cannot show the claim, do not say it.

## Write From Screen Truth

Before finalizing VO, scrape or review the actual screens.

Check:

- Labels.
- Numbers.
- Names.
- Statuses.
- Row counts.
- Dates.
- Output text.

Spoken numbers must reconcile with what appears on screen. If the VO says "five confirmed actions," the UI must show five confirmed actions.

## Timing Model

Render windows usually follow this shape:

- Lead: 0.2 to 0.4 seconds of silence or visual setup.
- Voiceover: the final audio clip duration.
- Tail: 0.5 to 0.8 seconds for the beat to settle.

The final voiceover clip length controls the scene window. If the voice changes or the line changes, remeasure and re-lock timing.

## Script Review Checklist

- Does every sentence have a visible actor?
- Does every number appear on screen?
- Is there one anchor object the viewer can follow?
- Does cause appear before effect?
- Does the beat end settled?
- Can a viewer understand the scene without reading small text?
