# Quality Rubric

Use this rubric before a film goes to stakeholders.

Score each area from 1 to 5.

## 1. Story Clarity

1: The film tours features without a clear question.

3: The problem is understandable, but the proof is scattered.

5: The film answers one business question through one visible loop.

## 2. Visual Acting

1: Screens sit still while narration continues.

3: Some phrases have actors, some are static.

5: Every sentence has exactly one visible actor.

## 3. Product Truth

1: The film claims things the UI does not show.

3: Most claims are visible, but some are implied.

5: Every claim is proven by a visible product state, number, or record.

## 4. Timing

1: Motion feels unrelated to the voiceover.

3: Major beats line up, but small actions drift.

5: Visual actions land on the spoken words.

## 5. Big-Screen Readability

1: Text is too small or dense.

3: Main ideas are readable, secondary areas are crowded.

5: A viewer can follow the film from the back of a room.

## 6. Aesthetic Discipline

1: Too many colors, effects, boxes, and labels.

3: The film is generally clean but has inconsistent emphasis.

5: One accent, clear semantic colors, restrained motion, and consistent hierarchy.

## 7. Visual Completeness

1: Scenes feel like placeholders: headline, blank space, and one thin visual.

3: Main scenes have useful detail, but some frames still feel underbuilt.

5: Every scene has purposeful visual density: hero object, support rail, proof detail, and settled composition.

## 8. IP Safety

1: Real names, brands, local paths, or private assets appear.

3: Most obvious leaks are removed, but provenance is unclear.

5: All text, prompts, assets, and examples are public-safe.

## Release Bar

Do not ship below:

- 4 in story clarity.
- 4 in visual acting.
- 4 in visual completeness.
- 5 in IP safety.
