# Start Here

This playbook helps you turn a product, workflow, launch, or concept into a short evidence-led film.

Start by choosing the signature format that fits the communication problem. The included presets cover portrait scroll stories, landscape launch films, and portrait collage explainers. A clean cut should always work on its own; voiceover and subtitle versions can be added when useful.

## The Core Idea

The proof should act while the narrator talks.

Each sentence needs one visual actor, whether that actor is real UI, kinetic type, a spatial chapter, or a collage object:

- A card appears.
- A number counts up.
- A pointer clicks.
- A status changes.
- A panel dims while another becomes active.
- A chat reply streams.
- A row moves from candidate to confirmed.

If nothing changes while a sentence is spoken, the viewer is being asked to listen and stare. That usually feels slow.

## The Production Loop

1. Define the business question.
2. Write a short narration.
3. Split narration into beats.
4. Choose one anchor object or persona to follow.
5. Choose the signature format and prepare its evidence.
6. Generate or record voiceover.
7. Transcribe voiceover to word timestamps.
8. Drive visual states from the timestamps.
9. Capture frames through a browser.
10. Stitch video and audio.
11. QC with frame grabs, contact sheets, text scans, and timing checks.

## Three Signature Formats

- Cinematic scroll story: a connected journey unfolds through portrait depth, continuity, and chapter reveals.
- Editorial launch film: a product, feature, or project gets a decisive landscape announcement with one memorable proof beat.
- VOX collage explainer: an abstract problem or mechanism becomes concrete through meaningful paper actors and assembled evidence.

Real UI, code-native graphics, and optional generated context are evidence treatments inside those formats—not competing formats. Keep real product proof wherever trust depends on seeing the workflow operate.

## First Scene Rule

Do not build the whole film before proving the standard.

Pick one representative beat. Make it excellent. Confirm the visual language, timing, and density. Then roll the system out across the rest of the film.
