# Worked Example: Acme Skills Platform

This is a complete fictional mini-production. It shows how the same idea travels from brief to beat sheet to anchors.

## 1. Brief

**Audience:** product and business leaders.

**Business question:** can a leader see a capability gap and understand the next action?

**Anchor object:** customer discovery capability gap.

**Proof point:** one dashboard gap becomes a focused plan with evidence.

## 2. Three-Beat Story

| Beat | Purpose | Visual setting | Actor |
|---|---|---|---|
| b01 | Ask the question | Framework opener | Film loop |
| b02 | Prove with product | Real UI | Gap card and evidence rail |
| b03 | Close the loop | Framework close | Proof pills |

## 3. Draft Voiceover

```text
Leaders do not need another activity report. They need to know where readiness is at risk.
The product surfaces the active gap, builds Jordan's next actions, and explains why each step belongs in the plan.
Now the outcome is visible: the business question, the personal evidence, the plan, and the record all stay connected.
```

## 4. Actor Map

| Phrase | Actor | Action |
|---|---|---|
| readiness is at risk | dashboard cards | cards count up |
| active gap | gap card | hot ring lands |
| next actions | plan rows | rows stage in |
| explains why | explanation panel | stream begins |
| outcome is visible | proof pills | pills activate |

## 5. Acceptance Gate

- Every sentence has one actor.
- The dashboard has evidence density, not placeholder emptiness.
- The closing frame shows the loop: question, evidence, plan, record.
- No private names, logos, screenshots, prompts, or real client data.
